# Ksetravid Website — Design Brainstorm

## Context
Ksetravid is a Progressive Death Metal / Tech-Death / Deathcore band from Bangalore, India. Their music blends brutal, progressive, and melodic aspects of modern metal. Lyrical themes: science fiction, manmade crisis, consciousness, Upanishads, false beliefs. Visual identity: intricate death metal logo with a central eye motif, dark/occult aesthetics, cosmic imagery.

## Idea 1 — "Sacred Geometry Brutalism"

**Design Movement:** Brutalist Web + Sacred Geometry + Death Metal Occult

**Core Principles:**
- Raw, uncompromising layouts with deliberate asymmetry and tension
- Sacred geometry patterns (mandalas, eye symbols) as structural elements
- Information hierarchy through scale contrast, not color softness
- Typography as weapon — aggressive, angular, oversized display text

**Color Philosophy:**
- Deep void black (#0A0A0A) as the primary canvas — the abyss
- Blood amber (#C8640A) as the primary accent — fire, danger, the third eye
- Bone white (#E8E0D0) for body text — aged parchment, ancient scrolls
- Rust red (#8B1A1A) for secondary accents — violence, sacrifice
- Emotional intent: oppressive, ancient, dangerous

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Full-bleed sections with no padding on edges — content bleeds to viewport
- Staggered, offset grid where text and images overlap intentionally
- Horizontal scrolling sections for discography
- Vertical timeline for band history

**Signature Elements:**
- The eye motif from the logo repeated as a watermark/background texture
- Geometric line art borders (not rounded, sharp angles only)
- Distressed/glitch text effects on section headings

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Cursor changes to a crosshair on hover
- Aggressive parallax on hero sections
- Section reveals with a "shatter" or "tear" animation

**Animation:**
- Entrance: elements slide in from extreme angles (not gentle fades)
- Hover: sharp scale-up with color inversion
- Scroll: parallax depth layers creating a sense of infinite void

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Metal Mania" or "Cinzel Decorative" — ancient, imposing
- Body: "IBM Plex Mono" — technical, cold, precise
- Accent: "Bebas Neue" — aggressive, condensed

## Idea 2 — "Cosmic Tech-Death Noir" ← SELECTED

**Design Movement:** Cosmic Horror + Technical Death Metal + Noir Photography

**Core Principles:**
- Deep space darkness punctuated by singular, intense light sources
- Technical precision in layout — grid-based but with deliberate breaks
- Photography-forward: band photos treated with high-contrast noir processing
- The "third eye" as a recurring visual metaphor — sight beyond sight

**Color Philosophy:**
- Near-black charcoal (#0D0D0D) as the void
- Electric crimson (#CC1A1A) as the primary accent — blood, danger, energy
- Cold steel (#8A9BA8) for secondary elements — technical precision
- Amber gold (#D4870A) for highlight moments — the third eye, sacred fire
- Muted off-white (#D9D4CC) for text — not pure white, aged and worn
- Emotional intent: cosmic dread, technical mastery, ancient wisdom

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Asymmetric two-column hero: logo/text left, full-bleed band photo right
- Diagonal section dividers (clip-path) to create dynamic flow
- Magazine-style discography grid with large album art
- Sticky navigation that transforms on scroll (transparent → solid dark)

**Signature Elements:**
- Noise/grain texture overlay on all sections for analog depth
- Red horizontal rule lines as section separators
- Band logo used as a large watermark behind hero content

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Smooth scroll with momentum
- Card hover: lift with red glow shadow
- Navigation links: underline draws from left on hover

**Animation:**
- Hero: staggered text entrance (each word slides up from below)
- Section reveals: fade-up with slight scale from 0.95 to 1.0
- Discography cards: parallax tilt on mouse move

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Oswald" (heavy weight) — imposing, condensed, powerful
- Body: "Source Serif 4" — readable, slightly editorial
- Accent/Labels: "Space Mono" — technical, cold, precise

## Idea 3 — "Vedic Darkness"

**Design Movement:** South Asian Occult + Death Metal + Manuscript Aesthetic

**Core Principles:**
- Ancient Indian manuscript textures fused with extreme metal brutality
- Mandala and yantra patterns as structural design elements
- Tension between the sacred and the profane
- Handcrafted feel with distressed, ink-like textures

**Color Philosophy:**
- Deep indigo (#0B0B1A) as the primary void — night sky, cosmic depth
- Saffron (#E8820A) as the sacred accent — fire, devotion, Hinduism
- Aged parchment (#C8B89A) for backgrounds of content sections
- Vermillion (#CC2200) for danger/aggression accents
- Emotional intent: ancient, sacred, terrifying, transcendent

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Vertical scroll with full-width "chapter" sections
- Decorative borders using geometric Indian patterns (not Celtic knots)
- Text set in columns like ancient manuscripts
- Album art displayed as "sacred artifacts" with ornate frames

**Signature Elements:**
- Yantra/mandala watermarks behind section content
- Ink-bleed text effects for headings
- Decorative dividers inspired by temple architecture

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Slow, meditative scroll speed
- Hover reveals hidden text like lifting a veil
- Transitions feel like turning pages of an ancient text

**Animation:**
- Entrance: elements materialize from smoke/mist
- Scroll: sections transition with a "page turn" effect
- Hover: subtle golden glow emanates from interactive elements

**Typography System:**
- Display: "Cinzel" — Roman-meets-Sanskrit gravitas
- Body: "Lora" — editorial, slightly ornate
- Accent: "Rajdhani" — South Asian geometry in letterforms

## Selected Design: **Idea 2 — "Cosmic Tech-Death Noir"**

This design best captures Ksetravid's duality: the technical precision of their music (tech-death, prog metal) and the cosmic/philosophical depth of their lyrics. The noir photography treatment honors their existing band photos, while the crimson-and-void palette echoes the Anamnesis artwork's dark intensity.
